### Water Properties

*   composition: H2O
*   polarity: it is a polar molecule
*   Hydrogen Bonding: water molecules from weak attractions called hydrogen bonds with
    one another and with other charged molecules

*   Cohesion: water molecules stick to each other
*   Adhesion: water molecules stick to other substances
*   Capillary Action: These two forces work together to allow water to flow 
    spontaneously through narrow spaces or porous materials
*   Surface Tension: water has a high surface tension, making its surface difficult
    to break and allowing some materials to rest on top of it
*   Freezing Expansion: unlike most substances, water becomes less dense when it
    freezes because it expands in its solid states
*   High specific heat: water resists temperature changes because it requires
    significant heat energy to raise its temperature
*   High heat of vaporization: it requires a lot of energy to evaporate, making it an
    effective evaporative coolant that carries away from surfaces
*   versatile solvent: water is excellent at dissolving ionic compounds and other
    polar molecules
*   Hydrophilic: Substances that dissolve easily in water 

*   Hydrophobic: Substances that dissolve difficultly in water
