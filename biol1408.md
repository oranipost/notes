*   The characteristics of life: 
    1.  Organization: Atoms makes up molecules which make up cells which make up

    2.  Energy use: A organism uses the energy from the Earth to fuel its own growth

*   Life requires energy

    Inside each cells, countless chemical reactions sustain life, which was called 
    "Metabolism"

    *   Primary producers (autotrophs): make their own food by extracting energy
    and nutrients from nonliving sources (such as from solar and chemical energy)

    *   Consumers (heterotrophs): obtain energy and nutrients by eating other 
        organisms, living or dead
    
    *   Decomposers (also heterotrophs): obsorb energy and nutrients from waste
        and dead organisms. Most of them are fungi and some bacteria, recycle
        nutrients to the nonliving environment

*   Life maintains internal constancy

    Homeostasis is the state of internal constancy

*   Life Reproduces, Grows, and Develops

    *   Reproduction transmits DNA from parent to offspring, often two ways:

        asexually and sexually

*   Life Evolves

    *   Adaptation is an inherited characteristic or behavior that enables an organism 
        to survive and reproduce successfully in its enviorment
    *   Natural selection is enhanced reproductive success of certain individuals
        from a population based on inherited characteristics
    *   Natural selection is one of mechanism of evolution
