##  Chapter 1: The Scientific Study of Life 

### Life

*   Celll is the basic unit of life and it can the living indivdual itself 

    Such as Paramecium

*   Every cell has an cell membrane that separate it from the outside.
*   DNA is the informational molecule of life.
*   Genes are the informational part of DNA and Cells use thes encoded instructions  
    in DNA to produce proteins.
*   The characteristics of life: 
    1.  Organization: Atoms makes up molecules which make up cells which make up 
        tissues and so on.
    2.  Energy use: A organism uses the energy from the Earth to fuel its own growth
    3.  Homeostasis: Your kidney regulate your body's water balance by adjusting
        the concentration of your urine
    4.  Reproduction, growth, and development: a seed grows into a tree and increases
        size, at maturity, reproduce sexually to produce its own seeds.
    5.  Evolution:  Increasing numbers of bacteria survive treatment with antibiotic
        drugs

*   Life is organized:

    1.  atoms
    2.  molecule
    3.  organelle
    4.  cell
    5.  tissue
    6.  organ
    7.  organ system
    8.  organism
    9.  population: includes members of same species occuyping the same place and 
        same time.
    10. community:  includes the population of different species in a region
    11. ecosystem:  includes both living and nonliving components of an area
    12. biosphere:  consists of all parts planet that can support life

*   Emergent properties are new functions that arise from interactions among a 
    system's componenets, like lots of brain cells can construct interacting 
    brain cells, contructing brain and memory ability

*   Life requires energy

    Inside each cells, countless chemical reactions sustain life, which was called 
    "Metabolism"

    *   Primary producers (autotrophs): make their own food by extracting energy
    and nutrients from nonliving sources (such as from solar and chemical energy)

    *   Consumers (heterotrophs): obtain energy and nutrients by eating other 
        organisms, living or dead
    
    *   Decomposers (also heterotrophs): obsorb energy and nutrients from waste
        and dead organisms. Most of them are fungi and some bacteria, recycle
        nutrients to the nonliving environment

*   Life maintains internal constancy

    Homeostasis is the state of internal constancy

*   Life Reproduces, Grows, and Develops

    *   Reproduction transmits DNA from parent to offspring, often two ways:

        asexually and sexually

*   Life Evolves

    *   Adaptation is an inherited characteristic or behavior that enables an organism 
        to survive and reproduce successfully in its enviorment
    *   Natural selection is enhanced reproductive success of certain individuals
        from a population based on inherited characteristics
    *   Natural selection is one of mechanism of evolution
