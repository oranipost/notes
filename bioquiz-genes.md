# Biology Practice: Inheritance, Genes, and DNA Replication

## Part 4: Complex Inheritance Patterns (20 Questions)

1. **In incomplete dominance, a cross between a red flower ($RR$) and a white flower ($WW$) produces:**
   A. All red flowers
   B. All white flowers
   C. All pink flowers
   D. Spotted red and white flowers

2. **Which of the following is an example of codominance in humans?**
   A. Skin color
   B. AB blood type
   C. Height
   D. Color blindness

3. **A trait controlled by many genes, such as human height or skin color, is called:**
   A. Monogenic
   B. Polygenic
   C. Epistatic
   D. Pleiotropic

4. **If a gene at one locus alters the phenotypic expression of a gene at a second locus, it is called:**
   A. Incomplete dominance
   B. Codominance
   C. Epistasis
   D. Linkage

5. **Sex-linked genes are located on:**
   A. Autosomes
   B. The X or Y chromosome
   C. Mitochondrial DNA
   D. Ribosomes

6. **Why are X-linked recessive disorders more common in males?**
   A. Males have two X chromosomes.
   B. Males have only one X chromosome, so one recessive allele causes the disorder.
   C. The Y chromosome carries a dominant allele that masks the X.
   D. Females cannot be carriers.

7. **A woman who is a carrier for color blindness ($X^BX^b$) marries a man with normal vision ($X^BY$). What is the probability that their son will be color blind?**
   A. 0%
   B. 25%
   C. 50%
   D. 100%

8. **In a pedigree, a circle represents a ____ and a shaded shape represents an ____ individual.**
   A. Male; unaffected
   B. Female; affected
   C. Male; affected
   D. Female; carrier

9. **If a trait skips generations and appears in both sexes equally, it is likely:**
   A. Autosomal dominant
   B. Autosomal recessive
   C. X-linked dominant
   D. Y-linked

10. **The phenomenon where one gene has multiple phenotypic effects (e.g., Sickle-cell disease) is:**
    A. Polygenic inheritance
    B. Epistasis
    C. Pleiotropy
    D. Codominance

11. **Human blood type AB is an example of:**
    A. Multiple alleles and Codominance
    B. Incomplete dominance
    C. Polygenic inheritance
    D. Recessive epistasis

12. **Which of the following blood types is the "universal donor"?**
    A. Type A
    B. Type B
    3. Type AB
    D. Type O

13. **Two genes are said to be "linked" if they:**
    A. Are on different chromosomes
    B. Are on the same chromosome and tend to be inherited together
    C. Code for the same protein
    D. Are both recessive

14. **The frequency of crossing over between two linked genes is higher if:**
    A. They are very close together.
    B. They are far apart on the same chromosome.
    C. One is dominant and one is recessive.
    D. They are on autosomes.

15. **A map of a chromosome based on recombination frequencies is a:**
    A. Karyotype
    B. Pedigree
    C. Linkage map
    D. Punnett square

16. **Nondisjunction during meiosis can lead to:**
    A. Point mutations
    B. Aneuploidy (e.g., Trisomy 21)
    C. Increased crossing over
    D. Faster DNA replication

17. **Which syndrome is caused by an $XXY$ genotype?**
    A. Turner syndrome
    B. Klinefelter syndrome
    C. Down syndrome
    D. Edwards syndrome

18. **A person with Turner syndrome has how many chromosomes?**
    A. 45
    B. 46
    C. 47
    D. 92

19. **Extranuclear (cytoplasmic) inheritance involves genes found in:**
    A. Golgi apparatus
    B. Mitochondria and Chloroplasts
    C. Ribosomes
    D. Lysosomes

20. **Which parent determines the sex of the offspring in humans?**
    A. The mother
    B. The father
    C. Both parents equally
    D. Environmental temperature

    B. 5' to 3'
    C. Left to right
    D. Top to bottom

    A. Primers
    B. Replisomes
