# Biology Practice: Mitosis, Meiosis, and Mendelian Genetics

## Part 1: Mitosis and Meiosis (20 Questions)

1. **The process of DNA replication occurs during which phase of the cell cycle?**
   A. G1 phase
   B. S phase
   C. G2 phase
   D. M phase

2. **During mitosis, when do the nucleolus and nuclear envelope disappear?**
   A. Prophase
   B. Metaphase
   C. Anaphase
   D. Telophase

3. **In a human cell ($2n = 46$), how many chromosomes are present during Metaphase of mitosis?**
   A. 23
   B. 46
   C. 92
   D. 44

4. **Which of the following is unique to Meiosis and does NOT occur in Mitosis?**
   A. DNA replication
   B. Separation of sister chromatids
   C. Synapsis of homologous chromosomes
   D. Formation of spindle fibers

5. **At the end of Meiosis I, the resulting two cells are:**
   A. Diploid and genetically identical
   B. Diploid and genetically different
   C. Haploid and genetically identical
   D. Haploid and genetically different

6. **The exchange of genetic material between non-sister chromatids is called:**
   A. Independent assortment
   B. Segregation
   C. Crossing over
   D. Fertilization

7. **Sister chromatids separate during which two stages?**
   A. Mitosis and Meiosis I
   B. Mitosis and Meiosis II
   C. Meiosis I and Meiosis II
   D. Prophase and Telophase

8. **If a parent cell has 24 chromosomes, how many will the daughter cells have after mitosis?**
   A. 12
   B. 24
   C. 48
   D. 6

9. **The alignment of chromosomes at the cell's equator occurs during:**
   A. Prophase
   B. Metaphase
   C. Anaphase
   D. Telophase

10. **A tetrad consists of:**
    A. Two sister chromatids
    B. Four homologous chromosomes
    C. Two homologous chromosomes (four chromatids)
    D. Four nuclei

11. **Which phase is characterized by the reappearance of the nuclear envelope?**
    A. Interphase
    B. Metaphase
    3. Anaphase
    D. Telophase

12. **The "reduction division" refers to:**
    A. Mitosis
    B. Meiosis I
    C. Meiosis II
    D. Cytokinesis

13. **In plant cells, cytokinesis involves the formation of a:**
    A. Cleavage furrow
    B. Cell plate
    C. Centriole
    D. Aster

14. **Independent assortment occurs during:**
    A. Metaphase I
    B. Metaphase II
    C. Prophase I
    D. Anaphase II

15. **Which protein complex holds sister chromatids together?**
    A. Centriole
    B. Cohesin
    C. Spindle
    D. Actin

16. **How many functional eggs are produced from one primary oocyte during oogenesis?**
    A. 1
    B. 2
    C. 4
    D. 8

17. **During Anaphase I of meiosis, what separates?**
    A. Sister chromatids
    B. Homologous chromosomes
    C. Non-homologous chromosomes
    D. The nucleus

18. **A cell spends most of its life in:**
    A. Prophase
    B. Cytokinesis
    C. Interphase
    D. Mitosis

19. **Genetic variation is increased by all of the following EXCEPT:**
    A. Crossing over
    B. Mitosis
    C. Independent assortment
    D. Random fertilization

20. **Meiosis II is most similar in mechanism to:**
    A. Meiosis I
    B. Mitosis
    C. Binary fission
    D. DNA replication

## Part 2: Mendelian Genetics (20 Questions)

21. **Mendel's Law of Segregation is based on the separation of alleles during:**
    A. Mitosis
    B. Gamete formation
    C. Fertilization
    D. Protein synthesis

22. **The genetic makeup of an organism is its:**
    A. Phenotype
    B. Genotype
    C. Locus
    D. Trait

23. **An organism with two identical alleles for a gene is:**
    A. Heterozygous
    B. Homozygous
    C. Hemizygous
    D. Hybrid

24. **In a cross $Aa \times Aa$, what is the probability of a homozygous recessive offspring?**
    A. 0%
    B. 25%
    C. 50%
    D. 75%

25. **The "P" generation in a genetic cross refers to:**
    A. The offspring
    B. The parents
    C. The second generation
    D. The pollen

26. **Which of the following represents a test cross?**
    A. $AA \times Aa$
    B. $Aa \times Aa$
    C. $A? \times aa$
    D. $aa \times aa$

27. **A dominant allele is one that:**
    A. Is always more common in a population
    B. Mask the expression of another allele
    C. Is "stronger" than a recessive allele
    D. Is only expressed in homozygous form

28. **If two white sheep produce a black offspring, the parent's genotypes must be:**
    A. Both $HH$
    B. Both $hh$
    C. Both $Hh$
    D. One $HH$ and one $hh$

29. **Mendel used which organism for his primary experiments?**
    A. Fruit flies
    B. Mice
    C. Pea plants
    D. Bacteria

30. **What is the phenotype of a plant with genotype $Tt$ (where $T$=tall, $t$=short)?**
    A. Tall
    B. Short
    C. Medium
    D. Half tall, half short

31. **The physical location of a gene on a chromosome is the:**
    A. Allele
    B. Locus
    C. Trait
    D. Centromere

32. **A cross involving only one character is called a:**
    A. Dihybrid cross
    B. Monohybrid cross
    C. Test cross
    D. Back cross

33. **Mendel's Law of Independent Assortment applies to:**
    A. Genes on the same chromosome (linked)
    B. Genes on different chromosomes
    C. Only sex-linked genes
    D. Only lethal genes

34. **If a child has type O blood and the mother has type A, the father could NOT be:**
    A. Type A
    B. Type B
    C. Type O
    D. Type AB

35. **The F2 generation is produced by:**
    A. Crossing two P individuals
    B. Self-pollinating or crossing F1 individuals
    C. Crossing a P with an F1
    D. Crossing two different species

36. **How many alleles does an individual usually carry for a single gene?**
    A. 1
    B. 2
    C. 4
    D. Varies by species

37. **In a cross $BB \times bb$, what percentage of offspring will be heterozygous?**
    A. 0%
    B. 50%
    C. 100%
    D. 25%

38. **A trait that is expressed only when two copies are present is:**
    A. Dominant
    B. Recessive
    C. Codominant
    D. Incomplete

39. **Mendel's work was largely forgotten until its rediscovery in:**
    A. 1865
    B. 1900
    C. 1953
    D. 1800

40. **A Punnett square is used to:**
    A. Determine the actual results of a cross
    B. Predict the probable outcomes of a cross
    C. Sequence DNA
    D. Observe chromosomes under a microscope

## Part 3: Ratios and Dihybrid Crosses (20 Questions)

41. **What is the expected genotypic ratio of a $Bb \times Bb$ cross?**
    A. 3:1
    B. 1:2:1
    C. 1:1
    D. 9:3:3:1

42. **In a dihybrid cross ($AaBb \times AaBb$), what is the expected phenotypic ratio?**
    A. 1:2:1
    B. 3:1
    C. 9:3:3:1
    D. 1:1:1:1

43. **A phenotypic ratio of 3:1 in the F2 generation indicates:**
    A. Incomplete dominance
    B. Complete dominance in a monohybrid cross
    C. Linked genes
    D. Epistasis

44. **In the 9:3:3:1 ratio, the "1" represents the proportion of:**
    A. Double dominant
    B. One dominant, one recessive
    C. Double recessive
    D. Heterozygous for both

45. **What is the phenotypic ratio of a test cross involving a double heterozygote ($AaBb \times aabb$)?**
    A. 9:3:3:1
    B. 1:1:1:1
    C. 3:1
    D. 1:2:1

46. **How many $1/16$ boxes in a dihybrid Punnett square represent the $AABB$ genotype?**
    A. 1
    B. 2
    C. 4
    D. 9

47. **If you cross $Rryy \times rrYy$, what is the probability of an $rryy$ offspring?**
    A. 1/2
    B. 1/4
    C. 1/8
    D. 1/16

48. **The 1:2:1 ratio in a monohybrid cross refers to:**
    A. Phenotypes
    B. Genotypes
    C. Gametes
    D. Chromosomes

49. **In a dihybrid cross, the "3"s in the 9:3:3:1 ratio represent:**
    A. Recombinant phenotypes
    B. Parental phenotypes
    C. Lethal genotypes
    D. Linked traits

50. **If 160 offspring are produced in a dihybrid cross ($AaBb \times AaBb$), approximately how many are expected to show both recessive traits?**
    A. 90
    B. 30
    C. 10
    D. 16

51. **What fraction of the offspring of an $AaBb \times AaBb$ cross will be $AaBb$?**
    A. 1/16
    B. 4/16
    C. 9/16
    D. 1/4

52. **A 1:1 phenotypic ratio is expected from which cross?**
    A. $AA \times aa$
    B. $Aa \times aa$
    C. $Aa \times Aa$
    D. $AA \times Aa$

53. **In snapdragons, red $\times$ white $\rightarrow$ pink. This 1:2:1 phenotypic ratio in F2 is due to:**
    A. Codominance
    B. Incomplete dominance
    C. Multiple alleles
    D. Pleiotropy

    A. The number of offspring
    B. The number of possible gamete combinations

    B. $aabb$

    A. 1:1
    B. 3:1
    C. 1:2:1

    A. 25%
