##  Chapter 3: Demand and Supply

### What is Demand and Supply

*   Demand refer to the amount of some good or service consumers are willing and able
    to purchase at each price
*   Demand is also based on ability to pay
*   If you cannot pay for it, you have no effective demand.
    
    Like someone who does not have a driver license has no effectiv to buy cars.


*   a buyer pays for a unit of the specific good or service is called price
*   the total of units that consumers would purchase at that price is called the 
    quantity demanded
*   the relationship between price and quantity demanded was called the law of demand
*   a table that shows the quantity demanded at each price a demand schedule
*   a demand curve shows the relationship between price and quantity demanded.

*   Supply means the amount of some good or service a producer is willing to supply
    at each price
*   A rise in price almost always leads to an increase in the quantity supplied of 
    that good or service
*   positive relationship between price and quantity supplied was called the law of 
    supply

*   Supply schedule shows the quantity supplied at a range of different prices
*   Supply curve isa graphic illustration of the relationship between price

*   If price is too low, the demand is larger than supply (shortage, excess demand)
*   If price is too high, the supply is larger than demand (surpluse, excess supply)

*   when both price and quantity of demand and price are the same, it is called the 
    equilibrium
*   equilibrium price is the only price where the demand is equal to supply

*   Ceteris parbus, means "other things being equal"

*   a product whose demand rises when income rises is a normal good

*   a product whose demand decreases when income rises is a inferior good

*   Some factors that shift demand curves:

    *   Changing tastes or preferences
    *   Changes in the composition of the population
    *   Changes in the pricesof related goods

        *   substitute is a good or service that we can use in place of another good 
            or service
        
        *   complements mean we often use the good together
    *   changes in expectations about future prices or other facts affect demand

        *   a shift in demand happens when a change in some economic factor causes
            a different quantity to be demanded at every price.

*   Laws that government enact to regulate prices are called price controls
*   A price ceiling keeps a price from rising above a certain level
*   A price floor keeps a price from falling below a given level

*   Consumer surplus is the aomount that individuals would have been willing
    to pay minus the amount that they actually paid

*   Producer surplus  is the extra benefit producers receive from selling goods or 
    services, measured by the price the producer actually  received minus the price
    theproucer would have been willing to accept

*   Social surplus (economic surplus, total surplus) is the sum of consumer surplus 
    and producer surplus

*   Deadweight loss is the loss in social surplus that occurs when the economy
    prouces at an ineffcient quantity
